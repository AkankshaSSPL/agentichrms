Q: What is the moonlighting disclosure policy?
Tools: search_policies
Sources:
  • NDA letter head copy.pdf | Page 4

Answer:
  The moonlighting disclosure policy requires employees to inform the
  company about any consulting work, directorships, or similar relationships
  with other entities during their employment. This disclosure is essential
  to avoid conflicts of interest and obligations. The company will treat
  such disclosures confidentially, aiming to prevent any potential conflicts
  between the employee's work for the company and their external
  commitments.  Additionally, employees must inform the company of any
  apparent conflicts related to confidentiality obligations or rights to
  inventions before engaging in outside work. Failure to disclose such
  information may lead the company to assume no conflict exists, and the
  employee may forfeit the right to make claims against the company
  regarding these matters.

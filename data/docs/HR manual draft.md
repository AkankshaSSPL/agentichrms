#### Working Days &Working Hours:

**Working Days**

*   **Saturday and Sunday** shall be observed as **weekly offs**, unless business exigencies require otherwise.

**Working Hours**

*   Working hours may be modified by the Company based on business requirements, with prior intimation wherever feasible.

**Lunch Break**

*   Employees/consultants shall be entitled to a **lunch break** between **\_\_\_ PM to \_\_\_ PM**, subject to work exigencies and operational requirements.
*   The timing and duration of the lunch break may vary depending on departmental needs.

**Work on Weekly Offs / Holidays**

*   In case of **urgent or critical business requirements**, employees/consultants may be required to work on **weekly offs or declared holidays**.
*   Such support shall be provided on a **priority basis**, subject to approval from the reporting manager and in accordance with company policy.

**Policy Administration**

*   The HR Department shall administer this policy.
*   The Company reserves the right to revise working hours or working days in line with operational or statutory requirements.

#### Leave Policy:

**Leave Year**

*   All leave entitlements shall be **calculated and governed on a Calendar Year basis**, i.e., from **1st January to 31st December**.

**Applicability**

*   This policy is applicable to all **confirmed employees and consultants**, unless otherwise specified in their engagement terms.

**Leave Application & Approval Process**

*   All employees/consultants must submit a **Leave Application** duly **approved and signed by the Department Head and HR Department (HRD)** at least **seven (7) days in advance** prior to proceeding on leave.
*   In case of **medical or other emergencies**, intimation must be given immediately via **phone call** to both the **HR Department and the Department Head**.
*   Upon resuming duty, the employee/consultant must submit the **Leave Application along with the relevant Medical Certificate**, wherever applicable.

**Leave Planning & Lapse**

*   Employees are entitled to 24 annual earn leaves (2 per month)
*   For Consultants working days less than 20 days, pay out to be deducted.
*   Employees/consultants are required to **plan their Privilege Leaves (PLs) in advance** and ensure that they are availed **within the same calendar year**.
*   **Accumulation of leave is not permitted**.
*   **Leave encashment is not allowed** under any circumstances.
*   Any unutilized leave at the end of the calendar year shall **automatically lapse**.

**Sick Leave & Medical Certificate**

*   In case of sickness exceeding **two (2) consecutive days**, the concerned employee/consultant must submit a **medical certificate** issued by a **Doctor or a Registered Medical Practitioner**.

**Compensatory Off (Comp-Off)**

*   Compensatory Off shall be granted only if the employee/consultant has worked for a **minimum of eight (8) hours** on a declared **weekly off or holiday**, with prior approval.
*   Approved Comp-Off must be availed **within fifteen (15) days** from the date of approval.
*   If not availed within the stipulated period, the **Comp-Off shall automatically lapse**.

**Leave Communication & Handover**

*   Employees/consultants must ensure that their **colleagues, seniors, and Heads of Department (HODs)** are informed well in advance about their leave.
*   All **key responsibilities and critical tasks** must be properly handed over before proceeding on leave.

**Leave Records & Leave Cards**

*   The Company shall provide a **Leave Card** to each employee/consultant for maintaining leave records.
*   **Separate leave cards** shall be maintained for each employee/consultant.
*   Leave cards must be **updated on a weekly basis or earlier**, as applicable, and are subject to verification by HR.

**Policy Administration**

*   The HR Department shall be responsible for administering and monitoring this policy.
*   The Company reserves the right to **amend, modify, or withdraw** this policy at any time based on organizational or statutory requirements.

**A.Maternity Leave**:-

**Policy Objective**

The Company is committed to supporting female employees during maternity by providing maternity benefits in accordance with the **Maternity Benefit Act, 1961**, as amended from time to time.

**Scope & Applicability**

This policy applies to all eligible female employees of the Company wherever the provisions of the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 are applicable.

**Eligibility Criteria**

A female employee shall be eligible for maternity leave and maternity benefit if she has worked with the Company for a minimum of **eighty (80) days** in the **twelve (12) months immediately preceding** the expected date of delivery.

**Duration of Maternity Leave**

*   An eligible employee is entitled to **twenty-six (26) weeks of maternity leave**.
*   Up to **eight (8) weeks** of maternity leave may be availed **before the expected date of delivery**, and the remaining leave after childbirth.
*   Maternity leave shall be granted strictly in accordance with the provisions of the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961.

**Maternity Benefit (Payment)**

*   During the maternity leave period, the employee shall be paid **maternity benefit**, and **not salary**, as prescribed under the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961.
*   The maternity benefit shall be paid at the **rate of the average daily wage**, as defined under the Act.
*   The benefit shall be paid through the Company’s regular payroll or statutory payment mechanism.

**Payment Timing of Maternity Benefit**

*   For the period of maternity leave **availed prior to the expected date of delivery**, the maternity benefit shall be paid **in advance**, upon submission of the required notice and medical certification.
*   For the period of maternity leave **after delivery**, the maternity benefit shall be paid **within forty-eight (48) hours** of submission of proof of childbirth, as prescribed under the Act.
*   Payment shall be made in instalments or lump sum, as per statutory requirements and company practice.

**Medical Bonus**

*   Where pre-natal confinement and post-natal care are **not provided free of charge by the Company**, the employee shall be entitled to receive a **medical bonus**, as prescribed under the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961.
*   The medical bonus shall be paid at the **statutory rate applicable at the time of delivery**, subject to submission of relevant medical documents, if required.

**Procedure for Availing Maternity Leave**

*   The employee must submit a written application to her reporting manager and the HR department, indicating the expected date of delivery and proposed maternity leave period.
*   Medical certification or proof of pregnancy/delivery may be required as per statutory provisions.
*   Any change in the maternity leave schedule must be promptly communicated to HR.

**Responsibilities of Reporting Managers**

Reporting managers are responsible for:

*   Intimating the HR department in a timely manner about employees who have availed or are expected to avail maternity leave.
*   Ensuring proper work handover and continuity of operations during the employee’s absence.

**Return to Work**

Upon completion of maternity leave, the employee shall be entitled to return to work in accordance with applicable laws and Company policies.

**Compliance with Law**

This policy shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the **Maternity Benefit Act, 1961**, and amendments made thereto from time to time. In case of any inconsistency, statutory provisions shall prevail.

**Policy Administration & Review**

The HR Department shall administer this policy. The Company reserves the right to amend, modify, or withdraw this policy to ensure continued statutory compliance.

**8) Whistle Blower Policy**

**Policy Objective**

The objective of this policy is to provide a transparent mechanism for employees and stakeholders to report genuine concerns about unethical behavior, misconduct, fraud, or violation of laws and Company policies, and to ensure that such concerns are addressed in a fair and timely manner.

**Scope & Applicability**

This policy applies to all employees of the Company, including permanent employees, probationers, trainees, consultants, and any other persons associated with the Company.

**Definition of Whistle Blowing**

Whistle blowing refers to the reporting of any suspected or actual improper activity, unethical conduct, fraud, or violation of legal or Company policies by an employee or any person associated with the Company.

**Reportable Concerns**

A whistle blower may report concerns relating to, but not limited to, the following:  
• Financial fraud or misrepresentation  
• Bribery or corruption  
• Misuse of Company assets or resources  
• Violation of Company policies or Code of Conduct  
• Breach of confidentiality or data protection obligations  
• Abuse of authority  
• Any act that may adversely affect the Company’s reputation or operations

**Protection of Whistle Blowers**

• The Company shall ensure that no employee who reports a concern in good faith shall be subjected to retaliation, harassment, discrimination, or adverse employment action.  
• Any act of retaliation against a whistle blower shall be treated as a serious disciplinary offence.  
• Protection under this policy shall not extend to disclosures made with malicious intent or false allegations.

**Confidentiality**

• The identity of the whistle blower shall be kept confidential to the extent possible, subject to the need for investigation or legal requirements.  
• All reports shall be handled with strict confidentiality and sensitivity.

**Reporting Mechanism**

• Whistle blowers may report concerns in writing to the designated authority, such as the Compliance Officer, Ethics Committee, or HR Head, through email or sealed written communication.  
• Anonymous complaints may be considered at the discretion of the Company, provided sufficient information is furnished to enable investigation.

**Investigation Procedure**

• All reported concerns shall be promptly reviewed and assessed.  
• An internal inquiry or investigation shall be initiated where deemed necessary.  
• The investigation shall be conducted in a fair, unbiased, and confidential manner.  
• The whistle blower may be contacted for additional information, if required.

**Action on Proven Misconduct**

• If the investigation confirms the reported misconduct, appropriate disciplinary or corrective action shall be taken in accordance with Company policies and applicable laws.  
• Actions may include warning, suspension, termination of employment, or legal proceedings, as deemed appropriate.

**False or Malicious Complaints**

• Any whistle blower who knowingly makes a false or malicious complaint may be subject to disciplinary action.  
• Such action shall be taken after due inquiry.

**Role of Management**

Management shall ensure:  
• Awareness of this policy among employees  
• Prompt and fair handling of whistle blower complaints  
• Protection of whistle blowers from retaliation

**Policy Administration & Review**

The HR Department or designated Compliance Authority shall administer this policy.  
The Company reserves the right to amend, modify, or withdraw this policy at any time to ensure compliance with applicable laws and best practices.

**11)** **PREVENTION OF SEXUAL HARASSMENT POLICY**

**Policy Objective**

The objective of this policy is to provide a safe, secure, and respectful working environment free from sexual harassment and to establish a mechanism for prevention, prohibition, and redressal of sexual harassment at the workplace.

**Scope & Applicability**

This policy is framed in accordance with the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 and shall be applicable to all workplaces of the Company.

**A) APPLICABILITY TO EMPLOYEES**

This policy shall apply to:  
• All permanent, temporary, probationary, contractual, and part-time employees  
• Trainees, interns, apprentices, and consultants  
• Employees working from office, client locations, or in remote/virtual work environments  
• Any person visiting the workplace, including vendors, customers, or clients

The policy covers incidents of sexual harassment occurring at the workplace, including but not limited to:  
• Office premises  
• Client or customer locations  
• Work-related travel  
• Virtual or digital work platforms  
• Any place visited by the employee during the course of employment

**B) PROCEDURE**

**Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)**

• The Company shall constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) in accordance with the provisions of the Act.  
• The ICC shall consist of a Presiding Officer, members from amongst employees, and an external member, as required by law.  
• Details of the ICC shall be displayed at the workplace.

**Filing of Complaint**

• An aggrieved woman may submit a written complaint to the ICC within three (3) months from the date of the incident.  
• In case of a series of incidents, the complaint may be filed within three (3) months from the date of the last incident.  
• The ICC may extend the time limit for filing the complaint if sufficient cause is shown.

**Conciliation**

• Before initiating an inquiry, the ICC may, at the request of the aggrieved woman, take steps to settle the matter through conciliation.  
• Monetary settlement shall not be the basis of conciliation.

**Inquiry Process**

• If conciliation is not requested or fails, the ICC shall conduct a formal inquiry.  
• The inquiry shall be conducted in accordance with the principles of natural justice.  
• Both parties shall be given a reasonable opportunity to be heard.  
• The inquiry shall be completed within ninety (90) days.

**Interim Relief**

During the pendency of the inquiry, the ICC may recommend interim measures, including:  
• Transfer of either party  
• Grant of leave to the aggrieved woman  
• Any other relief deemed appropriate

**Inquiry Report & Action**

• The ICC shall submit its inquiry report to the employer within ten (10) days of completion of the inquiry.  
• Based on the findings, the employer shall take appropriate action within sixty (60) days.  
• Actions may include warning, suspension, termination, or any other disciplinary measure as per Company policy.

**Confidentiality**

• The identity of the complainant, respondent, witnesses, and details of the proceedings shall be kept strictly confidential.  
• Breach of confidentiality shall attract disciplinary action.

**Protection Against Retaliation**

The Company shall ensure that no adverse action is taken against the complainant or witnesses for filing or supporting a complaint in good faith.

**False or Malicious Complaints**

• If a complaint is found to be malicious or knowingly false, the ICC may recommend appropriate action against the complainant.  
• Mere inability to substantiate a complaint shall not attract action.

**Policy Administration & Review**

This policy shall be administered by the HR Department and the Internal Complaints Committee.  
The Company reserves the right to amend or modify this policy in accordance with changes in law or business requirements.
